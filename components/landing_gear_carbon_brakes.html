Oleo-pneumatic shock absorber

The strut contains hydraulic fluid below and high-pressure nitrogen gas above. On touchdown, fluid is forced through a small orifice — the resistance absorbs the kinetic energy. Without it, the wing spar would snap on impact.

Bogie (6 wheels) — load distributed safely

Six wheels spread the load across a much larger footprint. The pressure at any point on the runway stays within safe limits. The bogie beam also helps on soft or uneven surfaces by self-levelling.

Carbon-carbon composite brakes

These discs are incredibly light yet absorb more thermal energy than any steel alternative. Under heavy braking they glow bright orange — exceeding 1,000°C — and can bring a fully laden A380 from 180 mph to a stop in under 25 seconds.
