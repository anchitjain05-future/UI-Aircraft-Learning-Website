The Role of the Tailplane
Explore what happens with and without the empennage
No Tail — Unstable

Without a tail: pure aerodynamic chaos

With no empennage, the aircraft has nothing to damp side-to-side swings. The nose whips left and right in an uncontrolled spiral called Dutch Roll — and any pitch disturbance sends it tumbling nose-over-tail.

Vertical stabilizer — preventing Dutch Roll (yaw)

The large upright fin at the tail acts like the feathers on an arrow. When the nose drifts sideways, the airflow hits the fin and pushes the tail back into alignment — automatically, with no pilot input needed.

Horizontal stabilizer — the downforce surprise

On most airliners the horizontal stabilizer pushes the tail down, not up. Because engines and fuel make the nose naturally heavy, the stabilizer acts like a see-saw — constantly pressing down on the tail to keep the nose level.
